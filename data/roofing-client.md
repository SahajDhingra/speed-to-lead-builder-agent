## 1. About / Overview

GenAIPI Roofing is a family-owned roofing and exterior contractor headquartered in McKinney, Texas, serving homeowners and small commercial property owners across the North Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex since 2009. We were founded by Cole Brennan, a former insurance adjuster, after one too many North Texas hailstorms left his own neighborhood scrambling for honest contractors. That adjuster background still shapes how we run: we read the storm report before we read the roof, we document damage the way a carrier actually wants to see it, and we don't oversell a roof that has another good decade in it.

We've grown from a two-truck operation working Allen and McKinney to a team of 30-plus across production, sales, and a dedicated insurance-claims desk, while staying deliberately regional. We're a GAF Master Elite contractor (top 2% of roofers nationally), an Owens Corning Platinum Preferred installer, and we carry full general liability and workers' comp on every crew. Most of what we do is storm and insurance work — wind and hail are the dominant business in North Texas — but we also handle straightforward cash repairs, full retail replacements, seamless gutters, and emergency tarping when a roof opens up at 2 a.m.

What we pride ourselves on is being the roofer that doesn't disappear. We answer the phone, we show up in branded trucks with named crews, we don't ask for a dime before materials are delivered, and we walk every claim with the homeowner so they're not negotiating with their carrier alone. Our reputation is built on the second roof, the referral, and the neighbor who saw our yard sign — not on chasing every storm three states away. We're licensed, local, and we still drive past most of the roofs we've built.

## 2. Services & Pricing

> Pricing reflects North DFW market rates as of 2026. North Texas runs slightly above national averages on labor due to demand spikes after hail events. All ranges assume architectural (dimensional) asphalt shingle unless noted; tile, metal, and synthetic priced on inspection. "Square" = 100 sq ft of roof surface.

### Roof Replacement (full tear-off & re-roof)
- **What it is:** Complete tear-off of existing roofing down to deck, deck inspection/repair, new underlayment, ice-and-water shield in valleys, drip edge, new shingles, ridge vent, and haul-off.
- **Price range:** $11,000 – $32,000 for most single-family homes (architectural asphalt). Per-square installed runs roughly **$575–$850/square** ($5.75–$8.50/sq ft). Larger or steep/cut-up roofs and premium "Class 4" impact-resistant shingles push toward the top.
- **Premium materials:** Standing-seam metal $25,000–$55,000+; concrete/clay tile $30,000–$70,000+; synthetic slate/shake $28,000–$60,000+.
- **Timeline:** Most asphalt re-roofs are a **1-day tear-off and install** once materials are delivered. Scheduling lead time is typically 1–3 weeks (longer in peak post-storm season). Insurance replacements pace to claim approval.

### Insurance / Storm-Damage Restoration
- **What it is:** Free storm inspection, damage documentation (photos, measurements, adjuster-ready report), claim filing support, adjuster meeting attendance, and full replacement or repair scoped to the approved claim.
- **Price to homeowner:** Typically **your deductible only** ($1,000–$5,000 depending on policy) when the claim is approved. We do not waive, rebate, or "eat" deductibles — that's insurance fraud in Texas and we won't do it.
- **Timeline:** Inspection within 24–72 hrs; claim-to-install commonly 2–6 weeks depending on carrier approval speed and supplement cycles.

### Roof Repair (cash / non-claim)
- **What it is:** Targeted fixes — replacing blown or cracked shingles, sealing exposed nails, small leak chases.
- **Price range:** **$350 – $1,800** for most common repairs. Minor patch jobs start around $350; multi-area or harder-access repairs run higher.
- **Timeline:** Usually scheduled within 3–7 business days; repair itself is a few hours.

### Leak Diagnosis & Flashing Repair
- **What it is:** Tracing active leaks, re-sealing or replacing flashing, chimney/skylight flashing, valley repair, vent boot replacement.
- **Price range:** Vent boot $125–$250; flashing repair $450–$1,200; valley repair $400–$1,600; full flashing replacement up to ~$2,000.
- **Timeline:** 3–7 business days to schedule (faster if active leak).

### Roof Inspection
- **Storm/insurance inspection:** **Free.** This is our front door.
- **Real-estate / point-of-sale certification inspection (with written report):** **$175 – $375**, credited back if you proceed to a paid repair or replacement with us.
- **Timeline:** Booked within 1–3 business days; report delivered within 48 hrs of inspection.

### Emergency Tarping / Board-Up
- **What it is:** After-hours temporary tarp on an open or actively leaking roof to stop water intrusion until permanent repair/claim.
- **Price range:** **$300 – $900** typical, billed at roughly $1.25–$2.75/sq ft for the tarped area, plus a **$150–$200 after-hours call-out fee**. Tarp cost is credited toward the permanent job if you proceed with us.
- **Timeline:** Same-day / within hours during business hours; after-hours dispatch by on-call crew (see §3).

### Seamless Gutters & Guards
- **What it is:** Custom on-site fabricated seamless aluminum 5"/6" K-style gutters, downspouts, and optional gutter guards.
- **Price range:** Seamless aluminum **$14 – $26/linear foot** installed; typical home **$2,400 – $5,500**. Gutter guards add **$8 – $20/linear foot**. Copper/specialty priced on request.
- **Timeline:** 1-day install; scheduled within 1–2 weeks.

### Exterior Add-Ons (storm-bundled)
- **What it is:** Siding repair/replacement, fascia & soffit, and window screens commonly damaged in the same hail/wind event and often claimable together.
- **Price range:** Scoped per project; siding repairs from ~$800, full elevations into five figures.
- **Timeline:** Coordinated with the roof claim/build.

## 5. How They Book & Dispatch

**Information we need from a lead (minimum to act):**
1. Full name + best callback number + email
2. Property address (confirms in/out of service area immediately)
3. What's going on — replacement, repair, leak, storm/insurance, gutters, inspection
4. **Is there active water coming into the home right now?** (triages emergency vs. scheduled)
5. Storm/insurance: has a claim been filed yet? Carrier name if known. Date of loss / storm date if known.
6. Property type (single-family / townhome / small commercial) and rough age of roof
7. How they found us (source)

**Booking flow:**
1. **Intake** (web form, call, Google LSA, referral) → logged in CRM, auto-acknowledged within minutes ("we've got it, a human will call you").
2. **Speed-to-lead call:** target first human contact **within 5–10 minutes** during business hours, next-morning for overnight leads. Active leaks call back immediately, day or night.
3. **Qualify & route:** confirm address is in-area, identify job type, flag insurance vs. cash, set urgency.
4. **Schedule inspection:** free for storm/insurance and most replacements; paid for cert inspections. Booked 1–3 business days out.
5. **Inspect & scope:** photos, measurements, drone/ladder as needed; written estimate or adjuster-ready report.
6. **Insurance path:** help file claim → meet adjuster on-site → reconcile scope/supplements → schedule build on approval & deductible collection.
7. **Cash path:** signed proposal + deposit-on-material-delivery (never upfront) → schedule build.
8. **Build & close:** 1-day install for most asphalt roofs → final walk → warranty paperwork → review request.

**Typical lead times:**
- Emergency tarp: hours.
- Inspection: 1–3 business days.
- Repair: 3–7 business days.
- Cash replacement: 1–3 weeks from signed proposal.
- Insurance replacement: paced to claim approval, commonly 2–6 weeks.
- Peak post-storm season (spring/early summer): all of the above stretch; we tell people honestly.

## 7. FAQs

**Q: My neighbors are all getting new roofs after the hailstorm. Do I have damage too?**
A: Quite possibly — hail tends to hit a whole neighborhood. The only way to know is a close-up inspection. Ours is free for storm damage, and we'll document everything so you have it whether or not you file a claim.

**Q: Will my insurance pay for a new roof?**
A: If your roof has covered storm damage and your policy is in force, it often will — that's the bulk of what we do. We can't speak for your carrier, but we'll inspect, document it the way an adjuster needs to see it, and walk the claim with you. You'd typically be responsible for your deductible.

**Q: How much is my deductible going to be?**
A: It's set by your policy, not by us — usually $1,000 to $5,000, and on many Texas policies it's a percentage of your home's insured value (often 1–2%). Check your declarations page; we can help you read it.

**Q: Can you waive my deductible or "work it into the price"?**
A: No. Waiving or rebating a deductible is insurance fraud in Texas, and we won't put you or us at risk. Any honest roofer will tell you the same.

**Q: How long does a roof replacement take?**
A: Most asphalt shingle roofs are torn off and replaced in a single day once materials are on site. Bigger, steeper, or tile/metal roofs take longer. The longest part is usually scheduling and, for claims, carrier approval.

**Q: Do I have to pay anything upfront?**
A: For cash jobs we don't collect a deposit until materials are delivered to your home — never before. For insurance jobs, you typically pay your deductible, with the balance handled through the claim.

**Q: There's water dripping into my house right now. What do I do?**
A: Call us — we have a 24/7 line for active leaks. Put a bucket down, move valuables, and we'll get a crew out to tarp it and stop the water. After hours there's a call-out fee, which we'll tell you before we roll.

**Q: Do you offer free estimates?**
A: Yes — storm inspections and replacement estimates are free. A formal written certification inspection (for a home sale, for example) runs $175–$375, and we credit it back if you do the work with us.

**Q: My roof is only 8 years old — could it really be damaged?**
A: Age doesn't make a roof hail-proof. We've replaced plenty of young roofs after a bad storm. Conversely, if your roof is genuinely fine, we'll tell you that too — we'd rather earn the next one.

**Q: What kind of shingles do you install?**
A: Mostly architectural (dimensional) asphalt from GAF and Owens Corning, including Class 4 impact-resistant shingles that can earn an insurance premium discount in Texas. We also do metal, tile, and synthetic on request.

**Q: Do you do repairs, or only full replacements?**
A: Both. If your roof has a lot of life left and just needs a repair, we'll do the repair. We don't push a replacement you don't need.

**Q: How long is the warranty?**
A: Workmanship warranty is 10 years on our installs. Manufacturer material warranties run 25–50 years depending on the shingle, and our Master Elite / Platinum Preferred status lets us register enhanced manufacturer warranties.

**Q: Do you install gutters too?**
A: Yes — custom seamless aluminum gutters and guards, fabricated on site. Hail and wind often damage gutters and roofing together, and they can sometimes be claimed together.

**Q: Are you licensed and insured?**
A: Yes — full general liability and workers' comp on every crew. Texas doesn't license roofers at the state level, but we carry voluntary RCAT certification and full insurance, and we're happy to send certificates.

**Q: What areas do you cover?**
A: The North DFW metro — McKinney, Frisco, Plano, Allen, Prosper, Denton, and the surrounding Collin/Denton/north Dallas county cities, roughly a 35-mile radius of McKinney. If you're outside that, let us know the address and we'll point you to someone good.

## 8. Ideal Customer & Lead Quality

### What a GREAT lead looks like
- **Insurance storm-damage full replacement, in-area.** Homeowner in our service ZIPs whose neighborhood was hit by a recent wind/hail event, roof is 5+ years old, owner-occupied single-family home, claim not yet filed or recently filed. This is the core of the business — high ticket, mostly carrier-funded, fast to close once damage is confirmed.
- **Signals:** mentions a storm/hail date, neighbors getting roofs, asks about insurance, gives a real address in-area, owns the home, reachable by phone.
- **Also great:** retail full replacement on a visibly aged roof in-area; multi-service storm bundle (roof + gutters + siding on one claim); a referral from a past customer.

### What a POOR / low-value lead looks like
- **Tiny cash repair, price-only.** "How much to fix two shingles?" with no interest in inspection — low ticket, high touch, often a renter or someone fishing for a phone quote.
- **Out-of-area.** Fort Worth, south Dallas, Tarrant County, out of state. We don't serve it and won't drive it.
- **Tire-kicker / price-shopper.** Wants a firm replacement number over email sight-unseen, comparing five bids, focused only on lowest price, no urgency, won't commit to an inspection.
- **Deductible-shoppers.** Asking us to waive/eat/"help with" the deductible — we decline these on principle.
- **Renters / non-decision-makers** with no owner authority.
- **Vague low-info blasts** with no address, no phone, "just checking prices."

### Signals that distinguish them
| Signal | Great lead | Poor lead |
|---|---|---|
| Address | In-area, specific | Missing or out-of-area |
| Job type | Storm/insurance replacement | Tiny cash patch / "just a quote" |
| Urgency | Active or recent storm, leak | None; "someday" |
| Insurance | Wants to file / already filed | Wants deductible waived, or N/A |
| Decision power | Owner-occupier | Renter / "asking for a friend" |
| Contact | Real phone + email | Email only / fake-looking |
| Intent | Wants inspection booked | Wants a number and gone |

**Routing rule of thumb:** In-area + (storm/insurance OR aged full replacement) + owner + reachable = call within minutes, book inspection. Out-of-area = polite referral. Tiny cash/price-only = give a range, offer paid inspection, low priority. Active leak = emergency path regardless of ticket size.
